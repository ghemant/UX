G.Hemant About Case Study

To design a Pop-Up store for Tata Elxi

About the project

The project, a Pop-Up concept exploration, was undertaken as Design Project in 3rd Semester at National Institute of Design, in collaboration with Tata Elxi and interdesciplinary teams were formed where participants were from Design for Retail Experience and Indteraction Design programme at NID, Bangalore Campus.
The task at hand was to design a Pop-Up store and the choice of product domain was open for the students to explore on their own.


To design an engaging experience, for urban citizens, to make sports fitness as part of their lifestyle.

My Team and My Role

We were a team of three students from two different disciplines at NID Bangalore. Omkar Khadamkar from Design for Retail Experience, Prabhakar Bind and I from Interaction Design. My contribution mainly involved in user and market research for health wearables and their sensors, which mainly comprised of secondary research - reading literary research papers, comparative study of existing wearables; and designing the mobile application,

Design Process

Research

We ideated about the possible ways to wear a health wearables, alongwith exploring different forms and mechanisms to design a wearable, which can measure all the required parameters.

To get a better understanding we studied available sensor technologies, which can be used for wearables, in the market. After finalising on the general idea of wearing the wearable, we started making thermocol mockup of the desired form.

Conducting primary research - Interviews, Contextual Inquiry and Observations.

Key Insight

Not beign FAT does not mean you are FIT.
